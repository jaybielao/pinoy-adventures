Major Regions

Luzon: The Northern Star

Luzon is the largest and most populous island in the Philippines, home to the capital city of Manila and many other cultural and natural attractions. It is also known as the Northern Star, because of its location in the northern part of the archipelago and its historical and political significance. Luzon is a land of diversity, with mountains, plains, coasts, and islands, as well as various ethnic groups, languages, and traditions. Luzon is a destination for travelers who want to experience the rich and vibrant Filipino culture and heritage.

Visayas: The Heart of the Archipelago

Visayas is the central part of the Philippines, consisting of several islands that surround the Visayan Sea. It is also known as the Heart of the Archipelago, because of its location and its cultural and natural diversity. Visayas is a destination for travelers who want to enjoy the stunning beaches, islands, and mountains, as well as the rich history, culture, and cuisine of the region.

Mindanao: The Land of Promise

Mindanao is the second largest island in the Philippines, with a population of about 25 million people. It is rich in natural resources, fertile soil, and diverse culture and history. It is also known as the Promised Land, because of its potential for development and prosperity. Mindanao is home to various indigenous and Muslim groups, who have resisted colonial and state oppression for centuries. Mindanao is also a destination for travelers who want to experience the beauty and adventure of its landscapes, wildlife, and festivals.
